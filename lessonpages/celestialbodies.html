Lesson: Celestial Bodies Presentation


Time: 5 class periods
Overview

For this project, I wanted to give students a creative way to compare and contrast two celestial bodies. Students will research 2 celestial bodies in our solar system, and then give a presentation comparing and contrasting those two bodies. The presentations are meant to be a little light hearted and fun to perform. Students are presented with 3 options for their presentations with a partner.

Purpose

Comparing and contrasting are important skills for analyzing and evaluating. Students need to learn about the physics of the solar system, but also need to review, and dive deeper into learning specifics about various celestial bodies.

Objectives

Students will present research comparing and contrasting celestial bodies

Indiana Standards

6.ESS.3   Compare and contrast the Earth, its moon, and other planets in the solar system, including comets and asteroids.

Materials & Resources
  • computer

Assessment Opportunities

A rubric will be used to evaluate the presentations and research.

Warm-up

To introduce this to the students, I setup a table and pretend to be a News Anchor. I have a projector screen behind me setup to look like a news stage background. In the voice of a News Anchor, I tell the students how they will give a presentation on celestial bodies; however, this time, they can 'perform' their presentation.

Activity

Direct Instruction

The Space Presentation Direction document has all necessary directions.

Guided Practice

At the beginning of this lesson I review the research practices that should align with the students ELA class. I provide an think-out-loud modeling of best practices for research at the middle school age.

Independent Practice

It is during this time that the class feels like a small workshop. The students working with their partners to conduct the research, create an outline, plan their presentation, and of course review and revise their work.

Warp-up

In reality, the "conclusion" of this lesson is brought about by the presentations. It takes roughly two 45 minute class periods to have each partner group present. Students who are "audience" members are required to take notes and be able to discuss the presentations given by their peers.
