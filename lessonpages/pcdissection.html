Lesson: PC_dissection


Time: 80 mins
Overview

There is nothing like a hands on experience to help promote curiosity. This lesson requires one major component that not all teachers will find easy to attain. To take this lesson on, you will need as many "old" computers as possible. Computers that are getting ready for recycling work perfect for this activity.

This lesson challenges students to research the device they will open up and improve their understanding of hardware/software connections. Their is a Lab paper connected to the lesson to help guide students through the process.

Purpose

Being able to explore the inside of the computer will help students further their troubleshooting skills. This hands-on experience, coupled with a research component, provides students with knowledge and an experience that they remember, helping them become independent problem solvers.

Objectives

Name major computer hardware components and explain its relationship to software.

Implement useful and incremental strategies in the problem solving process.

Indiana Standards

6-8.CD.1   Demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between hardware and software.

6-8.CD.2   Apply troubleshooting strategies to identify and solve routine hardware and software problems that occur during everyday computer use.

6-8.CD.3   Describe the major components and functions of computer systems and networks.

Materials & Resources
  • student computer
  • teacher computer & projector

Assessment Opportunities

Exit Question and Computer Dissection Lab paper

Warm-up

Students are directed to class conversation right away to maximize time for the lab. As a class, list some common problems each of us have encountered with computers. Then separate the list into categories of "software" or "hardware" problems. Take a moment to cover the two objectives listed for this lesson and how they are connected. Ask the students "How will and understanding of hardware help us with troubleshooting?" Let students share ideas.

Activity

Direct Instruction

This lesson is geared toward research and exploration. The teachers role is to provide guidance and structure to the exploration. Remind and review basic research skills with students prior to directing to begin research. Cover basic safety with a screwdriver and how best to work with their partner.

Guided Practice

Cover the PC Dissection Lab worksheet with the students. Make sure to model good search parameters to find quality information online.

  1. Research the Lenovo T440
  2. Unscrew back of computer, pull off covering, and pull of protective covers.
  3. Find and label the following parts on the computer
    1. Fan
    2. Processor
    3. RAM
    4. Hard Drive
    5. BIOS chip
    6. WiFi Network Card
    7. Internal Battery
    8. Monitor Connection
    9. Keyboard Connection

Independent Practice

Students will work on their own to complete the Computer Dissection Lab. The teacher will circulate to provide assistance and age appropriate explanations for hardware descriptions. When all students have successfully finished labeling AND writing the descriptions of the computer parts, the students will share their descriptions with at least one other student and see if they agree or disagree on the description being used for each part

Warp-up

There is an Exit Question included in the Lab. Students should complete the Exit Question and if time allows do a "turn and talk" with a different partner.

Exit Question from Lab:

You are trying to play some really awesome game on Cool Math, but the website continues to spin. You remembered that you have clicked about 15 different buttons on the website while the website was loading. You also noticed that the bottom of the computer feels really really hot. Using hardware part names, and troubleshooting strategies, explain what you believe the problem is and how you might solve it.
