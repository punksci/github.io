Lesson: Experimenting with Sound


Time: 80 mins
Overview

In this lesson students will investigate energy waves. Specifically sound waves, using a tuning fork, a stopwatch, and their own ear. The lab itself is highly structured and guided for use early in the school year.

Purpose

This lab serves two purposes. First, to have students investigate energy waves with a hands (or ear) on approach. Second, is to help teach students the scientific method and the process of investigating and conducting a lab. I use this lab early in the year to help students become engaged in the investigation process and to learn how to break problems down into smaller steps.

Objectives

Investigate how the properties of a medium affect energy(sound) waves.

Indiana Standards

6.PS.4   Investigate the properties of light, sound, and other energy waves and how they are reflected, absorbed, and transmitted through materials and space.

SEP.3   Constructing and performing investigations

Materials & Resources
  • computer
  • tuning forks
  • stopwatch
  • Transmitting Sound Lab
  • student worksheet 7.1 (graphing of data)
  • exit ticket/question
Assessment Opportunities

The Lab will be assessed for the understanding of the investigation process. The Exit Ticket will also be used to assess understanding.

Warm-up

Students have 3 words on the board that they need to research and define. The words are, amplitude, frequency, and wavelength. Review the lesson from the prior day that provided students with an online simulator to experience how energy waves move under various conditions.

Activity

Direct Instruction

Students will be directed to Google Classroom to find the instructions for today’s Lab. I will cover the basic structure of the lab and the Question/Problem we are solving in order to provide background for the students to create their hypothesis. Students will then create their hypothesis. Students will then be directed to the Experiment Procedures in the lab.

Guided Practice

Following along with the Experiment Procedures, I will model how to use the Tuning Fork and how to time the sound of the Tuning Fork. I will show students where to place the Tuning Fork for the “air” measurement and the “bone” measurement. I will explain to students how to fill out the Student Sheet 7.1 where they will track their data and create a graph. Students will be shown an example horizontal graph.

Independent Practice

Students will work with a partner to complete the Lab. One partner will time and the other will use the Tuning Fork. They will take turns back and forth until they each have 2 times for air and bone measurements, which will be written down on the Student Sheet 7.1. Students will then create a graph of the data and look for patterns to list for their Lab Observations. After students create their graph, they will share their graph with their partner to get feedback about their graph structure. Teacher will also circulate the room and provide guidance on the graphs. Once students have finalized their graph - students will then discuss 4 Analysis questions with their group. As they discuss the questions, and debate the answer, students will write their answer to each question in the Lab document. Analysis questions start at a DOK 1 and work their way up to a DOK 4 question asking about the relationship between the properties of a medium and the transmission of sound.

Warp-up

Teacher will lead discussion on Analysis questions. Groups will provide summary answers for each question. Misconceptions on the topic will be addressed by the teacher. Students will then be directed to answer an Exit Question through Google Classroom. The question assesses their understanding of the original question that was proposed for the lab: “How does changing the medium from a gas to a solid affect sound waves?”
