Hello

I'm Bryan Goeller

I am a multi-talented problem-solver with 14 years of experience helping students find their talent for critical thinking. I engage students with curiosity and an abundance of energy, creating an educational experience for any young scientist or engineer that will inspire growth. I'm currently teaching PLTW Design and Robotics classes for Eastwood Middle School; continuing my quest to inspire students to become excellent problem-solvers.

My time in education has been filled with an array of experiences. Community has been the central and driving theme of these experiences. From my time spent at Christel House Academy to my time spent teaching in my own neighborhood (at the time) of Irvington, 'community' has been essential to empowering students to tackle problems. While at Irvington, I taught 6-8 grade Computer Science, using a mixture of code.org, codeHS, and self-created lessons. The curriculum was focused on the Indiana State Standards and geared toward engaging kids with projects such as creating a game using JavaScript or designing and coding a website with HTML and CSS.

Beyond my experience as an educator, I did Tier 1 Tech Support for Irvington Middle School. This included helping manage 250 Chromebooks for the students and helping staff and students with standard technology troubles. My experience with web development has currently been limited to building a handful of websites, online courses through Codecademy, and my experiences teaching the skills of web development. With that being said, my experience in teaching has given me ample opportunity to debug code. I have not worked in the web development industry. My career focus has been on education. Nevertheless, as my career has moved forward, I have focused on Computer Science and Web Development in education. I continue building programs and web applications as a way to continue to improve myself and challenge myself to learn new skills.
